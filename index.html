Hack to End Homelessness is an initiative bringing together technologists, entrepreneurs, local businesses, policymakers, advocates, & service providers to end homelessness in our city.

Interested in volunteering, sponsoring, or otherwise getting involved? Please let us know:


Get involved as:

    We have three aims:

  • Educate the public about the causes of homelessness.
  • Inspire citizens to get involved.
  • Discover innovative solutions we can implement now.

Events:

Between May 1 and 6, 2014, we will host a series of events at Pioneer Square's 220 & Change that meet these objectives.

  • On Thursday, May 1, we will open our doors during Pioneer Square's ArtWalk for a photo showcase and panel discussion that reflect the realities of homelessness in our city.
  • On Friday, May 2, we will host a screening of @home, a documentary that follows social media advocate Mark Horvath of InvisiblePeople.tv on a cross-country exploration of homelessness.
  • On Saturday and Sunday, May 3-4, we will host the Hack to End Homelessness, where advocates, service providers, and technologists will collaborate to design projects. The weekend will culminate in a pitch event Sunday evening that will be open to the public.
